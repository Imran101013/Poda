PODA Pakistan Commends Historic Legislation Against Child Marriages in Punjab

14 February 2026 Child Protection

Thursday, 12 February, 2026: Sameena Nazir, Executive Director of Potohar Organization for Development Advocacy (PODA), warmly welcomes and commends the Government of Punjab for enacting historic legislation against child marriage in the province.

The Punjab Child Marriage Restraint Ordinance 2026, promulgated by the Punjab Governor, sets 18 years as the minimum legal age of marriage for girls and declares child marriage a non-bailable offence — a landmark step that PODA and its partner organisations have been advocating for over many years.

"This is a historic day for the girls and women of Punjab," said Sameena Nazir. "PODA has been working at the grassroots level for decades to end child marriage, and this legislation is a powerful validation of that work. We commend the Government of Punjab for taking this bold and necessary step."

PODA has been at the forefront of child marriage prevention in rural Punjab, running awareness campaigns, legal literacy workshops and community mobilisation programmes across multiple districts. The organisation has documented hundreds of cases where early marriage has been prevented through community intervention and legal advocacy.

PODA called on all stakeholders — including district administrations, law enforcement agencies, judiciary and civil society — to work together to ensure the effective implementation of the new law, particularly in rural and remote areas where child marriage rates remain highest.

The organisation also urged the government to invest in complementary measures, including girls' education, economic empowerment of families and awareness campaigns, to address the root causes of child marriage alongside the legal framework.
