Real Cases, Real Impact

Behind every policy and project update is a person. Two recent, named cases where community awareness and quick action stopped a child marriage and kept a girl's future open.

Nikah Khawan Prevents Child Marriage in Toba Tek Singh

District Toba Tek Singh, Punjab

A Nikah Khawan in District Toba Tek Singh demonstrated courage and integrity when a young couple approached him for marriage. Suspecting they were underage, he requested identification documents and discovered that the bride was only 16 years old and the groom was 17.

Despite pressure from the couple and their families, he refused to conduct the marriage, emphasizing the importance of following the law and protecting children's rights. His principled decision prevented an underage marriage and sent a strong message to the community about the importance of legal and responsible marriage practices.

"One person's commitment to justice and the law can help protect children and inspire positive change in society."

Stopping Early Marriage and Protecting a Girl's Future

District Chakwal, Punjab

Muqadas Arif was forced to leave school after Grade 5 and faced pressure from relatives to marry a 38-year-old man. Upon learning of the case, PODA Pakistan intervened by informing the local religious leader that Muqadas was underage and not legally eligible for marriage.

The team also mobilized community members, who raised the issue with local authorities, police, and media. As a result of these collective efforts, Muqadas's early marriage was stopped, safeguarding her rights and future.

"Community action and legal awareness helped protect a young girl from child marriage and gave her a chance for a better future."
