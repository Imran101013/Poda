A message from our Executive Director

Message from Leadership

Executive Director

Sameena Nazir

President, PODA Pakistan

2025 was a year our rural women's network turned into real policy change — years of legal-literacy work and grassroots advocacy helped shape the Punjab Child Marriage Restraint Ordinance, raising the minimum legal age of marriage for girls to 18. We also welcomed over 1,500 women leaders from 100 districts to our 18th Annual Rural Women Leadership Training Conference in Islamabad, and continued expanding the Rural Women's Network for Civic Leadership and Participation nationwide.

None of this happens without our donors, partners, staff and — most of all — the rural women, youth and communities who trust PODA to stand with them. As we head into 2026 and the launch of Beyond Barriers, our new disability-inclusion partnership with the European Union, I want to thank everyone who made 2025 possible.

Highlights of the year

What We Delivered in 2025

A selection of the projects and moments that defined the year — the full list runs to 49 projects since 2006, all documented on our Projects page.

  • Reduce Early Marriages to Enhance Gender Equality (Royal Norwegian Embassy, Dec 2022–Nov 2025) concluded a three-year campaign across all 41 districts of Punjab, helping shape the Punjab Child Marriage Restraint Ordinance 2026.
  • Capacity Building of Women Rights Defenders to Protect Rights (Czech Embassy, Mar 2023–Oct 2025) built a network of women rights defenders across 25 districts in four provinces.
  • Advancing Gender-Responsive, Inclusive, and Climate-Resilient WASH Policies (WaterAid, Jan–Mar 2025) convened consultations on climate-resilient WASH policy and women's leadership across Karachi, Lahore and Islamabad.
  • Hosted the 18th Annual Rural Women Leadership Training Conference in Islamabad (15–17 October 2025), bringing together 1,500+ rural women leaders from 100 districts.
  • Continued the Rural Women's Network for Civic Leadership and Participation (National Endowment for Democracy, May 2024–Jan 2026), expanding civic registration and leadership training nationwide.
Looking ahead

What's Next in 2026

April 2026 brings the launch of Beyond Barriers, a three-year partnership with the European Union and Sightsavers advancing disability inclusion and economic empowerment for women across Punjab and Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. The Rural Women's Network for Civic Leadership and Participation continues into 2026, and planning is already underway for the 19th Annual Rural Women Leadership Training Conference.
