PODA Press Release – December 2025

15 December 2025 Child Protection · Advocacy

PODA's latest statement on child protection and community advocacy efforts across rural Punjab and Khyber Pakhtunkhwa highlights the organisation's continued commitment to ending child marriage and protecting the rights of girls and women in Pakistan's most vulnerable communities.

Throughout 2025, PODA's field teams have been active in multiple districts, conducting awareness workshops, legal literacy sessions and community mobilisation activities focused on child marriage prevention. The organisation has worked closely with local government officials, community leaders and civil society partners to build a comprehensive response to the challenge of child marriage in rural areas.

PODA's programmes in Punjab and KP have reached thousands of community members this year, including parents, teachers, religious leaders and young people themselves. The organisation's approach combines legal advocacy with community-based education to create lasting change in attitudes and practices around child marriage.

As the year draws to a close, PODA reaffirms its commitment to continuing this work in 2026 and beyond, with a particular focus on strengthening community-based child protection networks and supporting the implementation of new legislation against child marriage.
