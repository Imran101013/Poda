PODA Call for Ending Early Marriages to Promote Healthier Families

12 December 2025 Child Protection · Women's Health

By News Desk — December 12, 2025, Murree: Ending child marriages strengthens families, as girls who marry at an appropriate age are physically and emotionally mature, equipped with the knowledge, skills, and resilience needed to build healthy, stable homes.

This was the central message of a workshop organised by PODA in Murree, where participants from across Punjab gathered to discuss the health, social and economic consequences of child marriage and to develop community-based strategies for prevention.

Speakers at the workshop highlighted that girls who marry before the age of 18 face significantly higher risks of maternal mortality, domestic violence and poverty. They are also less likely to complete their education, limiting their economic opportunities and their ability to contribute to their families and communities.

"When we end child marriage, we don't just protect one girl — we strengthen an entire family and community," said a PODA field coordinator. "Girls who marry at the right age are better mothers, better partners and better contributors to society."

The workshop brought together community leaders, health workers, teachers and young people to develop practical action plans for child marriage prevention in their respective communities. Participants committed to raising awareness, reporting cases and supporting girls who are at risk of early marriage.

PODA called on the government to strengthen enforcement of existing laws against child marriage and to invest in girls' education and economic empowerment as complementary strategies for ending the practice.
