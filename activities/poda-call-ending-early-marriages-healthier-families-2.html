PODA Call for Ending Early Marriages to Promote Healthier Families

10 December 2025 Child Protection · Advocacy

PODA reaffirms its commitment to ending child marriage and promoting the rights of girls and women to choose when and whom they marry, as part of its broader advocacy for healthier, stronger families across rural Pakistan.

This statement accompanies PODA's field activities in December 2025, during which the organisation conducted multiple workshops, community meetings and awareness sessions across Punjab and Khyber Pakhtunkhwa focused on child marriage prevention.

PODA's approach to ending child marriage is rooted in the belief that lasting change requires both legal reform and community transformation. While strong laws are essential, they must be accompanied by shifts in attitudes, norms and practices at the community level.

Through its Women Rights Education and Girls Right to Education work, PODA works with communities to build understanding of the harms of child marriage and to create supportive environments where girls can stay in school, develop their skills and make informed choices about their futures.

PODA also works with local leaders, religious figures and community elders to build consensus around the importance of girls' education and the need to end child marriage. These community-based advocates play a crucial role in changing norms and protecting girls at risk.

PODA calls on all stakeholders — government, civil society, communities and families — to join in the commitment to end child marriage and to invest in the education, health and empowerment of girls as the foundation for healthier, stronger families and communities.
