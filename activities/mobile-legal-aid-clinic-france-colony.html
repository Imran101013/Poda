Three-Day Mobile Legal Aid Clinic with the Support of NED, Islamabad

30 April 2026 Democracy & Human Rights Education

PODA, working in partnership with the National Endowment for Democracy (NED), organized a three-day mobile legal aid clinic from 28 to 30 April 2026, bringing free legal support directly to the France Colony community in Islamabad's F-7 sector. The initiative was designed around the needs of the neighbourhood's most vulnerable residents, with a particular focus on women and members of the Christian community who often face barriers in accessing formal legal channels.

On the clinic's final day, roughly 60 community members came forward seeking guidance on a range of legal concerns. A dedicated team of professionals — Ms. Farah Naz Awan, Mr. Khawaja Zahid Nasim, Ms. Aiman Zahra, and advocates Mr. Attiq ur Rasool and Mr. Nisar — provided one-on-one consultations, referred cases to the appropriate institutions, and led educational sessions on fundamental rights and legal procedures.

A central theme throughout the three days was the Christian Marriage (Amendment) Act 2025, which sets the minimum marriageable age at 18 years for both parties. The team distributed informational materials to help residents understand the changes and what they mean for families in the community, reinforcing the clinic's broader goal of building lasting legal awareness rather than one-off assistance.

PODA's leadership used the closing of the clinic to reaffirm its commitment to the concerns raised by France Colony residents, pledging to help establish a Mohalla Committee that will work alongside the community on legal identity documentation, sanitation, and other civic issues that surfaced repeatedly over the three days.
